The Government of Odisha has announced that vehicles with a valid Pollution Under Control Certificate (PUCC) will soon be required to carry green stickers issued by the Transport Department.

Odisha Transport Minister Bibhuti Jena explained that the initiative aims to strengthen road safety and environmental compliance. He warned that vehicles without valid pollution certificates will face strict action.

The minister stated that preparations have already begun ahead of Road Safety Month. He added that the fines increased in 2019 will be reviewed, and necessary adjustments may be made after comparing penalties with other states.

According to the minister, both new and old vehicles will receive certification to ensure uniform compliance across the state. He emphasised that the Transport Department will directly supply the green stickers to vehicle owners.

Officials highlighted that the move will make it easier to identify vehicles meeting pollution norms and encourage others to update their certificates regularly, contributing to cleaner air and safer roads.
